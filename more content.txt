Madhuri Sakhi Sametha Sri Premika Varadan
Madhurapuri Ashram is located in the picturesque, serene Maharanyam village. It is in the
outskirts of Chennai, a few kilometers from Sriperumpudur, the birth place of the great
Vaishnava Acharya, Sri Ramanujacharya. Madhurapuri has a significant and close relation with the
two avatars of Bhagavan - Sri Rama and Sri Krishna. Legend has it that Sri Rama spent three days
in this village as He journeyed southward in search of Sita Devi. The neighbouring Manimangalam
village has a very ancient Rajagopalaswami Temple in which stone inscriptions can be found
referring to this region as ‘Dwaravati’ which means ‘Dwaraka’. The citizens of Dwaraka, from
Sri Krishna’s time, were settled in this village by Sage Agastya. Mahatmas say, even today,
they have vision of Rishis doing tapas underneath the ashram.
The essence of Madhurapuri Ashram is Bhagavata Dharma, Bhagavan Sri Krishna and Nama Kirtan.
Inside the Ashram, the beautiful kutir Madhuvanam is the abode of Sri Swamiji. Kunja Bhavanam
accommodates the Ashramites and Veda patasala students. Sant Ekanatha Swami Dining Hall is a
massive hall where annadaanam is performed for all devotees who visit the ashram.
Sri Sandeepani Gurukulam Veda patasala is an important part of the ashram premises.

BHAGAVATA BHAVANAM
Aptly named by Sri Swamiji, Bhagavata Bhavanam the ashram satsang hall is a place for incessant Mahamantra kirtan and satsangs. The Durbar of Sri Swamiji’s pooja moorthi, Madhuri Sakhi sametha Sri Premika Varadan (Sri Krishna-Sri Radha Rani) seated in their jhoola in an ornately crafted wooden mantap sanctifies the hall. Devotees chant Mahamantra from 6 am to 6 pm everyday, and circumambulate Namam Ketkum Perumal, the deity who stands majestically at the centre of the hall. This chanting commenced on 8th November 2006.

KANYAKUMARI SRI JAYA HANUMAN
Kanyakumari Sri Jaya Hanuman stands majestically adjacent to Sri Kalyana Srinivasa Perumal Temple. Hanumanji is 24 feet tall,
symbolizing 24,000 slokas of Valmiki Ramayana. Since the day of consecration, 26th February 2009 – Mahashivaratri Day,
numerous devotees throng to have His darshan and experience positive changes in their lives. Vadamaalai (garland of vadas), vennai kaappu (butter) and betel leaf garland are offered for
fulfillment of prayers. It is considered special to have darshan of this Hanumanji on any specific day of the week for 7 consecutive
weeks and offer a garland of betel leaves upon conclusion of the 7 weeks.

Govindapuram is a beautiful town between the cities of Kumbakonam and Mayiladuthurai, on the banks of the holy Kaveri river. The jiva samadhi of
Sri Bhagavannama Bodhendra Saraswati Swamigal, who established the Nama Siddhanta, is located here. 'Chaitanya Kuteeram', named after

Mahamantra happens here from 6 am to 6 pm every day. Daily pooja is done as per Bhagavata Dharma. Annadhaanam is performed here.

Namadwaar
Namadwaar, meaning
'doorway to the Divine Name' is a centre for Nama Kirtan.
Numerous Namadwaars have been established round the globe with
Madhuri Sakhi Sametha Sri Premika Varadan as the presiding deity.
Prayers from devotees are submitted at the sanctum sanctorum
and Mahamantra chanting takes place in these centres.
These centres also organize social and cultural events in their
respective localities.

Kalpatharu day
Kalpatharu Day Mahamantra Mass Prayers are conducted on the 1st of January every year commemorating the Kalpatharu Day when Swami Ramakrishna Paramahamsa showered blessings on all His disciples like a wish-fulfilling tree. This event takes place in the divine presence of Sri Swamiji in different places every
year, and is attended in person and online by thousands of devotees across the globe.
